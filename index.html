Safe Multi-Robot Navigation in Expanding Hazard Environments

I Made Putra Arya Winata, Sangcheol Oh, Junghyun Oh*
Lab: RAIL KWU

Abstract

Safety is a central requirement in multi-robot navigation, and substantial progress has been made on collision avoidance among robots and with static or moving obstacles. However, less attention has been given to hazards whose geometry expands over time, as in hazardous environments where fire, gas, or contamination zones spread and continuously shrink the safe region. In this paper, we study decentralized multi-robot navigation in the presence of growing obstacles modeled as time-varying expanding sets. We formulate admissible (safe) sets for obstacle avoidance, inter-robot collision avoidance, and their combination, and derive safety guarantees under mild assumptions. Finally, we validate the proposed framework in simulations for teams with $N\ge 3$ robots, demonstrating collision-free goal reaching in environments with expanding obstacles.

Overview

We consider decentralized multi-robot navigation with individual goals under growing obstacles (expanding hazard regions). The controller stack combines goal-seeking behavior with safety layers to avoid both inter-robot collisions and time-varying obstacle violations.

  • Growing-obstacle reach–avoid: model expanding hazards as time-varying constraints.
  • HJ safety levels: compute pairwise safety values for robot–robot interactions.
  • MIP coordination: assign which robot avoids which to resolve multi-robot conflicts.
  • Safety shield: enforce obstacle invariance near the expanding boundary.
