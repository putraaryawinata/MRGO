Safe Multi-Robot Navigation in Expanding Hazard Environments

I Made Putra Arya Winata, Sangcheol Oh, Junghyun Oh*
Lab: RAIL KWU

Abstract

Safety is a central requirement in multi-robot navigation, and substantial progress has been made on collision avoidance among robots and with static or moving obstacles. However, less attention has been given to hazards whose geometry expands over time, as in environments where fire, gas, or contamination zones spread and continuously shrink the safe region. In this work, we study decentralized multi-robot navigation in the presence of growing obstacles modeled as time-varying expanding sets. We formulate admissible (safe) sets for obstacle avoidance, inter-robot collision avoidance, and their combination, and provide safety guarantees under mild assumptions. Simulations with $N\in\{3,4,5,6,7\}$ robots demonstrate collision-free goal reaching in expanding obstacle environments.

Overview

We consider decentralized multi-robot navigation where each robot has an individual goal while operating under expanding hazard regions (“growing obstacles”). The controller stack combines goal-seeking behavior with safety layers to prevent both inter-robot collisions and violations of time-varying obstacle constraints.
